Strahan & beyond

The west coast
has a lot to offer.

On the water
Cruise
Gordon River Cruise
200m · 10 min walk
A full day on the Gordon River through World Heritage wilderness. Departs Strahan wharf. Book ahead in summer.
Walk
Strahan Foreshore
2 min walk
The boardwalk along Macquarie Harbour. Morning light on the water. Best before 8am when the tour boats arrive.
Beach
Ocean Beach
12km · 15 min
The longest beach in Tasmania. Wild, windswept, empty. Driftwood as far as you can see. Not for swimming.
In the wilderness
Dunes
Henty Dunes
20km · 20 min
Enormous sand dunes behind Ocean Beach. Surreal landscape. Walk to the top for views over the Southern Ocean.
National Park
Cradle Mountain
195km · 2.5hr
One of Australia's great walks. The Overland Track begins here. Day walks from Dove Lake are extraordinary.
Rainforest
Franklin-Gordon Wild Rivers
Surrounds Strahan
World Heritage listed. One of the last great temperate wildernesses on earth. The river cruise takes you through it.
Eat & drink
Restaurant
The Strahan Village
5 min walk
The main dining option in town. Seafood, local produce. Book ahead for dinner in peak season.
Café
Hamer's Hotel
3 min walk
Heritage pub, cold beer, simple food. The oldest continuously licenced hotel on the west coast.
Bakery
Strahan Bakery
4 min walk
Pies, pastries, proper coffee. Essential for the morning before a day on the river.
History
Heritage Rail
West Coast Wilderness Railway
Departs Strahan
The rack railway from Strahan to Queenstown. One of the great heritage rail journeys in Australia.
History
Macquarie Harbour Penal Station
Sarah Island · Boat access
Australia's most brutal convict settlement. Accessible by cruise. The Gordon River cruise passes the ruins.
Town
Queenstown
41km · 40 min
The old mining town. Lunar landscape, colourful hills, world-class mountain biking. Completely unlike anywhere else.
