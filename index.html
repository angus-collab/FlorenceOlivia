Heritage · 2 Bedrooms · 4 Guests

A place the west coast made.

Strahan sits at the edge of the Gordon–Franklin wilderness — the most intact temperate rainforest in the southern hemisphere. Florence Olivia is a restored heritage building from the 1890s mining boom, watching the harbour since before anyone living remembers.

The Huon pine floor was there when we arrived. So was the pressed tin ceiling. Both are still there. We added beds, a wood fire, and left the rest alone.

When to visit

The west coast in every season.

Spring
Sep — Nov
7°C – 16°C
Best for walks

The bush is flowering. Henty Dunes and coastal walks at their best. Fewer crowds, lower rates.

Summer
Dec — Feb
11°C – 21°C
Peak season

Long light until 9pm. The harbour at its calmest. Book ahead — 3 night minimum applies.

Autumn
Mar — May
9°C – 17°C
Most dramatic

The crowds are gone. The light turns amber through the rainforest canopy. Ideal for the Gordon River.

Winter
Jun — Aug
4°C – 12°C
Most intimate

Storms off the Southern Ocean. Fires, whisky, and rain on the windows. The west coast at its most itself.
