The Property

Built in the 1890s.
Restored for now.

The place

Strahan sits at the edge of the Gordon–Franklin wilderness — the most intact temperate rainforest in the southern hemisphere. To get here you drive through mountains, past button grass plains, and beside rivers the colour of tea. Most people don't make it this far. That's the point.

Florence & Olivia is a restored heritage building from the 1890s mining boom. It watched the town grow from a port into a village and watched it quiet again. The Huon pine floor was there when we arrived. So was the pressed tin ceiling. Both are still there.

The experience

A stay at Florence & Olivia is about stillness. There's no noise unless you make it. The harbour is two minutes on foot. The Gordon River cruise departs 200 metres away. Cradle Mountain is three hours if you want to push north.

We added beds, a wood fire, a proper kitchen, and left the rest alone. The pressed tin ceiling is still there. The Huon pine floor creaks in exactly the right way. The light through the windows in the morning is the same light it's always been.

The detail

2 bedrooms. Queen bed in the main, twin singles in the second. Sleeps 4. Minimum 2 nights — 3 nights in summer. Self check-in. Full kitchen with everything you need to cook properly. Wood fireplace in the living room.

We stock the basics — coffee, tea, a small welcome from the west coast. Everything else is yours to fill.
